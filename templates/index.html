Section 138 FAQ


About Section 138


Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, deals with the offense of dishonor of a cheque for insufficiency of funds or if it exceeds the amount arranged to be paid by the drawer's account. The drawer of the dishonored cheque can face legal consequences, including penalties and imprisonment.


This section is crucial in maintaining the integrity of financial transactions and upholding the credibility of negotiable instruments.

  • It provides a legal framework for addressing cheque dishonor cases.
  • Penalties may include fines and imprisonment.
  • Proper understanding and compliance with Section 138 are essential for financial transactions.
